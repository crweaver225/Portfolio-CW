**The Terminator: The Skynet Funding Bill is passed. The system goes on-line August 4th, 1997. Human decisions are removed from strategic defense. Skynet begins to learn at a geometric rate. It becomes self-aware at 2:14 a.m. Eastern time, August 29th. In a panic, they try to pull the plug.
Sarah Connor: Skynet fights back.
The Terminator: Yes. It launches its missiles against the targets in Russia.
John Connor: Why attack Russia? Aren't they our friends now?
The Terminator: Because Skynet knows the Russian counter-attack will eliminate its enemies over here.**

HollyWood has a long and treasured history of telling stories of humanity playing God with computers and foretelling the potentially species ending consequences of their actions. I grew up watching movies like *The Invisible Boy*, *Colossus: The Forbin Project*, and *Terminator*. These movies have a common theme; AI systems become "self aware" and purposefully put into action their plans for world domination and/or the extermination of the human species. While sufficient for a two hour summer blockbuster, this author will be the first to push back against the notion that the majority of the most treasured parts of the human experience such as consciousness and self-awareness are things modern day AI systems can be said to have[^1]. Nor are they requirements for the dangers of AI I wish to speak to. LLM's have exploded onto the scene in the last five years in a manner that is quickly turning Hollywood science fiction into reality; this is true. But its dangers are less like those depicted in Terminator and more like those found in older stories such as *The Monkeys Paw*.

[^1]: I am not making the bold claim that I have solved the problem of other minds. My ego might be big, but its not that big. My claim is more a probabalistic one that is probably worth a deep dive in the near future to flesh out.
